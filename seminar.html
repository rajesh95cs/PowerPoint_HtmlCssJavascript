INTRODUCTION

  • Technologies such as wireless sensor networks,shortrange wireless communications,and radio-frequency identification (RFID) have allowed the Internet to penetrate in embedded computing
  • The rise of multisensory mobile phones with Internet connectivity has helped to reduce the barriers for associating mobile computing with the IoT/WoT

Internet Of Things

  • Internet of thing is that things can communicate to each other without human.

  • Internet of things targets to connect various objects (e.g. mobile phones, computers, cars, appliances) with unique addresses, to enable them interacting with each other and with the world.

Mobile Phone Computing and IOT/WOT

Application Catagories

Participatory Sensing

Applications that enable individuals in the general public to gather, analyze and share local information

Application

  • Noise Tube Project[1]

Noise Tube Project

  • GOAL : Help monitor noise pollution wherever you live.

  • TASK : Measure and map your daily noise exposure with a mobile app.

  • DESCRIPTION :

    • Developed in Sony Computer Science Lab
    • Facilitates sound measuring at any place and any time through a mobile app which exploits basic smartphone functionalities, namely microphone, wireless connectivity and localisation through GPS.
    • NoiseTube website collects all user measurements and visualises them on Google Maps.
    • Resulting maps give an average but not at all a complete view on the situation.
    • Free and Open Source.

Noise Tube Project

Eco Feedback

Involves mobile applications that provide feedback to the users about various environmental phenomena or events, or about their personal consumption

Application

  • UbiLense[2]

UbiLense

  • GOAL : Help monitor energy consumption of electrical and electronic devices.

  • TASK : Measure energy consumed by an electrical device just by pointing on them using a phone camera

  • DESCRIPTION :

    • Mobile phone sends the camera image to the image recognition server.
    • Server returns name and associated Plogg ID of the recognized object.
    • Mobile phone requests current energy consumption from associated Plogg.

UbiLense

Discussions and Issues

  • HETEROGENITY:
    • It is because of the Iot devices/services that provides propreitary communication protocols
    • Needs regarding the data quality and content always change from person to person
    • Some users need real time information and others need historical data
    • So IoT and mobile phone computing need to be open to support diverse applications and requirements.
  • CONTINUOS SENSING:
    • Some IoT/WoT-enabled mobile applications require continuous sensing, signal processing, analysis and inference which asks for more computation, memory, storage, sensing, and communication bandwidth.
    • Sending the compressed summaries over the air (rather than raw data)
  • CROWD SENSING:
    • How can the sensing opportunities and sensing quality be measured?
    • How to deal with incomplete, noisy and unreliable data?
    • How many mobile users can provide enough sensing opportunities to achieve the required sensing quality?
    • How to tackle the fact that people always move around a set of popular locations, instead of purely random movements?
  • CONTEXT PROBLEM:
    • This occurs because phones may be exposed to events for too short a period of time.
    • The sensors might requires more time to gather a sample.
    • Phone calls could also interfere in collecting relevant context.
  • SECURITY:
    • Top priority in mobile computing, and becomes more important when interacting with the IoT.
    • Ensuring security of shared resources against misuse by unauthorized mobile users.
  • PRIVACY:
    • Privacy in terms of sharing personal information through the Web is one of the most important research challenges.
    • Privacy threats have been reported in various mibile applications
    • Create authentication proxy between the user and the conected device
  • RELIABILITY:
    • Reliability relates how well a mobile phone can measure precisly and identify anomalies.
    • Faults in data can create false alarms.
  • SEARCH AND DISCOVERY:
    • It is crucial for locating nearby, local and relevant real-world devices and people exploiting their services for the creation of more advanced knowledge.
    • The WoT has not yet standardized any technique for real time discovery of physical entities and their services
  • PERSONALIZATION:
    • It is expected to give significance to the interaction with the IoT/WoT through mobile computing
    • Planty could understand the user’s sense of aesthetics and help him create his personal garden.
    • Sports apps could record the personal characteristics and achievements of the users to suggest realistic short and long-term goals.
    • Mobile computing, personalized to a user’s profile, empowers him/her to make more informed decisions across a spectrum of WoT-enabled services.
  • EMOTIONS ANALYSIS:
    • It relates to personalization as it allows to offer better services to users according to their reactions in relation to the features provided by the mobile applications.
    • Research in this area involves sensing conscious and unconscious emotions , aiming to quantify and log aspects of our behavior we are not aware of.
  • PERSUASION:
    • Persuasion in mobile computing is an open area, creating design elements and exploiting various psychological/sociological factors which affect users to change their behavior toward better quality of living, more sustainable life, environmental awareness, etc.
    • The connection between mobile computing and IoT/WoT offers great new opportunities for examining which design characteristics of mobile phone applications and which motivational factors may influence users to question and change their behavior and way of living.
  • BIG DATA ANALYSIS:
    • It is a necessary step in most participatory sensing applications, in order to extract useful information from vast amounts of real-world data streams.
    • An open, standard methodology for IoT analytics problems is still nonexistent.
  • BUSINESS CASES:
    • These are required to prove the market potential of the IoT/WoT and mobile computing
    • Merging of digital and physical world through Web technologies and mobile computing is profitable to enterprises worldwide.
    • Evrythng is a start-up company aiming to demonstrate, through various case studies , the economic viability of IoT based investments.
    • Solutions offered : brand protection, inventory management, consumer loyalty, data monitoring and analysis, etc.

CONCLUSION

  • The practice of combining mobile computing and the IoT/WoT seems to offer tremendous new opportunities in many real-life domains
  • Enabling the seamless integration of devices, services and information that can create advanced knowledge, more informed reasoning and decision-making,as well as encouraging big data analysis, more use of persuasive and engagement techniques, emotions analysis and personalization
    • .
  • However, this openness comes together with various risks in privacy, security and reliability of information,and also various challenges
